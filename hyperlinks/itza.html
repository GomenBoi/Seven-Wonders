Chichén Itzá

Chichén Itzá was a large pre-Columbrian city built by the Maya people around the Mexican Yucatán Peninsula. It is regarded to many architecture workers as a great masterpiece and work of art, and there are many speculations to the cause of Chichén Itzá's downfall.

Origin and Purpose

The large city of Chichén Itzá is a Mayan ruin which was once a large centre of trade, economics and politics. It was built around 400 AD, and lasted until 900 AD, largely to factors such as overpopulation, disease, lack of resources, etc.

Population

Chichén Itzá hosted over 50,000 people during its time as a large city, and it was said that it was quite diverse.

Dimensions

The site of Chichén Itza covered 4 square miles of terrain (10 square kilometres), the temple itself has a height of 24m and a square base, width of 54m.

Construction Details

The majority of the pyramid and other buildings such as the Temple of the Warriors and the Obversatory were made out of stone, but there was other various materials such as limestone chiseled from nearby quarries known as cenotes.
