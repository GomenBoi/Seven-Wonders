The Colosseum

The Colosseum of Rome is a large arena or amphitheatre used for mainly entertainment, sports and viewings of performances. Moreover it is the largest standing amphitheatre to this day, which makes it into one of the Seven Wonders of the World.

Origin

The Colosseum originated sometime around 70 to 72 AD and was originally named the Flavian Amphitheatre under the Flavian emperors.

Purpose

As mentioned before, the purpose of the Colosseum of Rome was for entertainment and sports, this led to large scale gladiator fights also being hosted within the Colosseum, where they fought against themselves and animals as well.

Construction

The dimensions for the colosseum were a short axis of 156m, and a long axis of 188m. It is an elliptical structure compromised mainly out of stone, concrete and tuff which was used to construct the external walls, stairs and radial walls.

Main Figures

The roman emporer at the time was Vespasian and his son, Titus, who also ordered the building of the Colosseum of Rome. It was considered a gift to the people and the pinnacle of Roman architectural strength.

Destruction

In 1349, a large earthquake inflicted severe damage upon the Colosseum, causing it to collapse. After this, the Colosseum continued to be a target for raw materials.
