Machu Pichu

Machu Pichu is located in Peru and is considered a magnificent urban architectural creation by the Inca empire and is regarded as one of the Seven Wonders of the World.

Origin

Machu Pichu was believed to be a royal estate or a sacred religious site for Inca leaders, and the inhabitants were members of the Inca tribe. It is located within the Cusco region of Peru and is surrounded by mountainous terrain and ruined buildings as of present time.

Purpose

The purpose of Machu Pichu was to house the Incan emperor at the time, Pachacuti, and is regarded as high importance by many archaelogists as the "Lost City of the Incas" as it is familiarised as the icon of Incan civilisation.

History

Since Machu Pichu was built at the height of the Inca Empire, it was prosperous and the Incas successfully established an economial structure surrounding agriculture production with cross-community exchange. However, the Incas had met their demise, supposedly due to the Spanish invaders.

Discovery

The discovery of Machu Pichu was hosted by the american archaelogist, Hiram Bingham, who arrived in Peru to originally find Vilcabamba but instead stumbled upon the ruins of Machu Pichu and the intricate stone terraces which were the entrance to the city.

Temple of the Sun

The Temple of the Sun in Machu Pichu is a religious site, which has an elliptical design similar to a sun temple found at the Inca capital of Cuzco. It was believed that it served as a worship area where inhabitants would go to pray.
