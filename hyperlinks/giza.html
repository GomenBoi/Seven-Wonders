The Great Pyramid of Giza

The Great Pyramid of Giza was an ancient monument built by the Eyptians in roughly 2550 to 2490 BC, it was regarded as elite tombs for the ruling pharaohs at the time.

Origin

The Pyramids of Giza were a pyramid complex and are part of the oldest monuments to date built by the Eyptians and are the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the World.

Purpose

The Great Pyramid of Giza specifically was built as a tomb for the Fourth Dynasty Eyptian pharoph Khufu during the 26th century BC.
